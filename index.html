REACHING THE UNREACHED

PURPOSE

We believe that every child has an equal opportunity at life

HOW

Vaccines save lives, it’s simple as that, however, for vulnerable children in poor resource, poor infrastructure settings, access to lifesaving vaccines can mean the difference between having a chance to live or death. Immunization therefore represents one of public health’s most valuable and cost-effective interventions, and delivers positive health, social, and economic benefits. A key barrier that prevents the distribution and access to lifesaving vaccines to majority of children 0-5 years especially from vulnerable, poor remote communities has been lack of proper identification and registration mechanisms. Putting the child at the center of the tracking efforts is not as simple, as majority of children especially in poor infrastructure and poor resource settings as well as remote and vulnerable communities have no formal record of their existence. By helping health workers to properly identify children chanjoplus enables children most at risk especially from vulnerable poor communities to access lifesaving vaccines and adhere to full immunization schedules thereby protecting them from vaccine-preventable health burdens. Leaving no one behind starts with knowing who everyone is. Last mile access to lifesaving vaccines is achievable when we can put the faces behind the numbers. “Children are still dying because of measles, polio, diarrhoea, and pneumonia – diseases that can be prevented and should not be causing deaths now. Getting every child access to vaccines translates into healthy lives for families: it means poverty reduction and greater access to education.” Collince Osewe;Founder

CHANJO PLUS FEATURES

Fromm Registration of Infants to Immunization Data Analytics Chanjoplus:

  • Registration

    Clinic is pre-registered by Chanjo+ on the platform

  • Vaccination

    The clinic receives information showing previous vaccines given, the age of the child and vaccine due. User registers via USSD using birth ID number

  • Confirmation

    Success message sent to patient and clinic staff once the record is updated.

  • Analytics

    Clinic staff can view all patient information and track immunization through web application NGO’s and Government can see macro level data through a separate portal

  • Technology

    Chanjoplus is a USSD powered vaccine record. It creates a unique digital identifier for every child and tracks their progress as they receive vaccinations. The platform is built to integrate into existing health systems directly.

  • Last Mile Delivery

    Partnering with community health workers, Chanjoplus aims to leverage existing delivery networks to create and update the vaccine records as soon as the vaccine is administered, creating a real-time view of vaccine coverage throughout the country.

  • Data & Insights

    A web platform enables clinic staff to view patient information, a centralized portal for NGOs and Governments provides macro level reports and planning tools.

  • Extension Services

    To solve the issue of missed appointments the team provide parents and caregivers with SMS alerts and information of vaccine programmes.

RESOURCES

  • Immunization Equity

    Health inequities are the unjust differences in health among different social groups. Unfortunately, inequities are the norm, both in terms of health status and access to, and use of, health services. Childhood immunizations reduce the incidence of vaccine-preventable diseases and represent a cost-effective way to foster health equity. This paper reflects a 2015 review of data from surveys conducted in developing countries from 2005 to 2011 that show significant inequities in immunization coverage and discusses several initiatives currently underway (including Gavi, the Vaccine Alliance) that are directed at increasing childhood immunizations or reducing or abolishing overall health inequities. These initiatives have already had a significant impact on disease burden and childhood mortality and give rise to optimism that health disparities may further be reduced and health equity achieved as a result of investments made in immunization.

  • Immunization Needs a Technology Boost

    Vaccination coverage is still below the Healthy People 2010 and 2020 goals. Technology use in the US is widespread by patients and providers including text message, email, internet, social media and electronic health records. Health information technology (IT) interventions can facilitate the rapid or real-time identification of children in need of vaccination and provide the foundation for vaccine-oriented parental communication or clinical alerts in a flexible and tailored manner. There has been a small but burgeoning field of work integrating IT into vaccination interventions including reminder/recall using non-traditional methods, clinical decision support for providers in the electronic health record, use of technology to affect work-flow and the use of social media. The aim of this review is to introduce and present current data regarding the effectiveness of a range of technology tools to promote vaccination, describe gaps in the literature and offer insights into future directions for research and intervention.
